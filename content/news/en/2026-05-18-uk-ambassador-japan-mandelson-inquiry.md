# UK's Next Japan Ambassador May Be Called to Testify in Mandelson Inquiry

**Corin Robertson**, Britain's designated next ambassador to Japan, could be summoned to give evidence in an inquiry examining matters related to **Peter Mandelson**, according to reporting from [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2026/may/18/uk-ambassador-japan-corin-robertson-mandelson-inquiry). The potential summons highlights how diplomatic appointments can intersect with ongoing political investigations in the United Kingdom.

The development suggests that Robertson's appointment to one of Britain's most important Asian diplomatic posts may be complicated by his potential involvement in the inquiry. While details about the specific nature of the investigation remain limited, the situation underscores the scrutiny that senior government figures and appointees face during periods of political inquiry.

## Background on the Inquiry

The Mandelson inquiry represents a significant political investigation in the UK. Peter Mandelson, a prominent Labour politician who served in various senior government roles, has been the subject of official examination. The inquiry's scope and specific focus are matters of public record, though the exact details of Robertson's potential involvement have not been fully disclosed in available reporting.

Robertson's potential testimony suggests he may have relevant knowledge or documentation related to matters under investigation. His designation as ambassador to Japan indicates he holds significant standing within UK diplomatic circles and government, making his potential evidence particularly noteworthy.
