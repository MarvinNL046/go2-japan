# Trump Backs Japan's Takaichi in Meeting with Xi Jinping

According to reporting by the Yomiuri newspaper and confirmed by [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/trump-throws-weight-behind-japans-takaichi-meeting-with-xi-yomiuri-says-2026-05-24/), former US President Donald Trump expressed support for **Sanae Takaichi**, a prominent Japanese political figure, during a meeting with Chinese President **Xi Jinping**. The reported endorsement underscores the complex diplomatic dynamics between the United States, Japan, and China, with Trump's backing potentially signaling American commitment to Japan's political leadership amid regional tensions.

The specifics of when and where this meeting took place are not detailed in the available reporting, but the Yomiuri's account suggests Trump used the occasion to reinforce US-Japan relations at a time of heightened geopolitical competition in the Indo-Pacific region.

## Who Is Sanae Takaichi?

**Takaichi** is a senior figure in Japanese politics known for her conservative positions and advocacy for strengthened Japanese defense capabilities. Her prominence in Japan's political landscape has made her a notable voice in discussions about regional security, particularly regarding China's military expansion and territorial claims in the East China Sea.

Trump's reported backing of Takaichi carries symbolic weight, as it suggests the former US president views her as a reliable partner for advancing American interests in Asia. Such endorsements can influence domestic Japanese politics, where international support—particularly from the United States—carries significant political currency.

## US-Japan-China Diplomatic Triangle

The reported meeting and Trump's comments reflect the ongoing triangular relationship between Washington, Tokyo, and Beijing. Japan has long served as a critical US ally in Asia, hosting American military bases and cooperating closely on security matters. However, Japan also maintains complex economic and diplomatic ties with China, requiring careful balancing.

Trump's explicit backing of Takaichi during discussions with Xi may be interpreted as a signal that the United States prioritizes its alliance with Japan and supports Japanese leaders who take a firmer stance on regional security issues. This positioning aligns with broader US strategy in the Indo-Pacific, where maintaining strong partnerships with democratic allies has become increasingly central to American foreign policy.

The timing of such diplomatic moves is rarely coincidental. By publicly supporting Takaichi through his meeting with Xi, Trump may be attempting to influence both Japanese domestic politics and China's calculations regarding regional power dynamics.

## What This Means for Japan's Political Landscape

Trump's reported endorsement could have implications for Japanese domestic politics, particularly if Takaichi is being considered for higher office or greater influence within Japan's government. International backing from a former US president—especially one with significant influence over Republican politics—can enhance a politician's domestic standing and credibility.

For Japan's government and people, the reported meeting highlights the continued importance of the US-Japan alliance in shaping regional security policy. It also underscores how decisions made in high-level diplomatic meetings between world leaders can have ripple effects across Asia.

## What's Next

The full details of Trump's comments regarding Takaichi and the broader context of his meeting with Xi remain limited based on current reporting.
